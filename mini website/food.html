About

A tagline for a Filipino cuisine encyclopedia could be "A Culinary Journey Across the Archipelago, From Island Flavors to Ancestral Roots", emphasizing regional diversity and rich history.

The "regional" aspect of Filipino cuisine refers to the distinct dishes and cooking styles found across different islands and provinces, such as spicy Bicolano dishes, lechon from Cebu, or the vegetable-centric Ilocano cuisine.

The "disease" aspect relates to potential public health concerns like high fat and cholesterol levels in the Filipino diet, which the country's Food Safety Act aims to address by setting standards for food quality and consumption.
