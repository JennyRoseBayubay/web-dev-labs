    Ingredients:

    • 3.5 oz dried taro leaves
    • 6 cups coconut milk
    • 2 cups coconut cream
    • 1/2 cup shrimp paste (bagoong or balaw)
    • 1/2 lb. pork shoulder, thinly sliced
    • 5 to 7 pieces red chilies
    • 1 piece onion, sliced
    • 1/2 cup sliced ginger
    • 8 cloves garlic, crushed

    Instructions:

    1. Combine the coconut milk, pork, shrimp paste, ginger, onion, and garlic in a cooking pot. Heat the pot and let boil.
    2. Once boiling, gently stir to mix the ingredients. Cover and simmer for 15 to 20 minutes, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking.
    3. Add the dried taro leaves. Do not stir. Let them sit and absorb the coconut milk for about 20 to 30 minutes. You may gently push them down.
    4. Once absorbed, stir the leaves gently and cook for another 10 minutes.
    5. Pour in the coconut cream and add the red chilies. Stir and cook for 10 to 12 minutes.
    6. Transfer to a serving plate. Serve hot.
    7. Share and enjoy!
    Bicol Express (Bicol Region) Baboy sa gata na may maanghang na sili, isang Bicolano classic!

    Ingredients:

    • 2 lbs. pork belly, sliced into strips
    • 2 cups coconut milk
    • 2 cups coconut cream
    • 1/4 cup shrimp paste or salted krill (bagoong alamang)
    • 5 cloves garlic, crushed
    • 5 pieces Thai chili pepper, chopped
    • 2 thumbs ginger, minced
    • 1 piece onion, chopped
    • 2 pieces Serrano pepper, sliced
    • 1 cup water (optional)
    • 8 grams Maggi Magic Sarap

    Instructions:

    1. Combine pork, ginger, garlic, onion, Thai chili pepper, Serrano pepper, shrimp paste, and coconut milk in a pan. Mix well.
    2. Cover the pan and turn the heat on. Let the mixture boil.
    3. Stir and adjust the heat to low. Cover and simmer for 50 minutes. Add water if needed to prevent drying.
    4. Add the coconut cream and more shrimp paste if desired. Season with Maggi Magic Sarap.
    5. Continue cooking over low heat until the sauce thickens.
    6. Transfer to a serving plate and enjoy with warm rice!
